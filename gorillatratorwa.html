    3 days Budget Gorilla Trekking Safari Bwindi Overview:
    3 Days Budget Gorilla Trekking Safari Tour Bwindi: trek – and closely observe – endangered mountain gorillas in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest National Park. Bwindi Impenetrable Forest is the best place in Uganda to track mountain gorillas this is because the park protects half the world’s population of these rear mountain gorillas and has several habituated groups. The forest is also a birdwatcher’s paradise- with 350 species recorded, including many Albertine Rift endemics.

    On this 3 days bwindi Budget Gorilla Trekking Safari, takes you to an adventure through the misty thick jungles of Africa’s most diverse forest, Bwindi Impenetrable Forest situated along the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) border and next to the Virunga National Park of Rwanda and on the edge of the Albertine Rift. Trekking the endangered Mountain Gorillas and their families.

    What to Expect of the 3 days Bwindi Budget Gorilla Trekking tour
    Bwindi is a pristine rainforest on the edge of the Albertine Rift Valley. The terrain is a string of ridges and valleys covered in very dense impenetrable forest. The main attraction we will be looking out for in Bwindi is its mountain gorillas. Of its population of approximately 459 individuals, more than a hundred are habituated. Surprisingly, Bwindi has more than 120 mammal species, which is the 2nd most of any park in Uganda (after Queen Elizabeth NP). Most of these are small forest species. Elephant is the only one of the Africa big 5 which is present, although they are rarely seen. More regularly encountered are some of the 6 antelope species, which include bushbuck and several types of duiker.

    The 3 days Bwindi Budget Gorilla Trekking Safari
    The 3 days bwindi budget gorilla trekking safari starts and Ends in Entebbe/Kampala. From your hotel, we’ll drive south-west of the capital Kampala for 7+hours to Kisoro District just out of Bwindi Impenetrable Forest National Park. Along the way you’ll have a stopover at the Equator where we will have an opportunity to stand in 2 hemispheres at the same time.

    The drive takes us through savannah grasslands seeing banana plantations and herds of Long-horned Ankole cattle with their incredibly long curved horns. Then we ascend into the Kigezi highlands, known as the “Switzerland of Africa”, with terraced farming and mountain scenery to Bwindi Impenetrable Forest.

    Day breaker !
    The third day of the the Bwindi Budget Gorilla Trekking safari:- this is the most adventurous day of the safari/tour, because you get to embark on a real jungle trek through the misty thick forest. But gorilla trekking is no stroll in the park. It can take anywhere up to 10 hours to find the elusive creatures in the dense undergrowth.

    Guides will lead trekkers up precipitous verges and across rivers, and rusty machetes are used to hack paths through the thick, thorny rain-forest. It is the perfect place to live out a childhood Tarzan fantasy, with vast swathes of trees, vines, branches and bushes surrounding trekkers as they penetrate deep into the rain-forest.

    Key Note
    When we finally find the gorillas, you’ll spend up-to 1 hour in there presence because that is how much time we are allowed to spend with them. You’ll be required to follow a couple of rules like no flash on your cameras, no sudden noises, no sneezing directly to the animals and other guidelines for a successful trek…. enquire with our team for more info.

    Important Information
    Best time for this 3 days Bwindi Budget Gorilla Trekking : Rainfall is the main factor to consider when deciding the best time for gorilla tracking. Heavy rains result in slippery and challenging hiking trails. It can rain at any time of the year, but the time periods of June to August and December to January have less rain and are thereby considered the best time to go. However, The park stays open throughout the year.
